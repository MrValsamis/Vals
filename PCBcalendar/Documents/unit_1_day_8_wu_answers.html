Problem 2. Red and Blue Shifts

  • Red Shift: When a sound source is moving away from the observer, the observed frequency is lower than the actual frequency. In the context of light, this is associated with the red end of the visible spectrum.
  • Blue Shift: When a sound source is moving towards the observer, the observed frequency is higher than the actual frequency. In the context of light, this is associated with the blue end of the visible spectrum.
